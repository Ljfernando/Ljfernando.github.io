About Me

Welcome to my website! I am a student at the University of San Francisco majoring in Data Science with a concentration in Computational Analytics. As an aspiring Data Scientist I enjoy diving head first into datasets and exploring the treasures within. I am deeply interested in the fields of Data Mining, Data Visualization and Applied Statistics. Aside from data-related ventures, I also take pleasure in music. I grew up learning how to play various instruments from the percussion, brass and string families (sorry woodwinds) and singing in different genres of music from Dixieland Jazz to Musical Theater. I am also an avid cyclist which helps to keep an active lifestyle and to feed my hunger for adrenaline. After earning a my Bachelor's degree, I hope to continue my studies towards a Master's degree in Analytics to further enhance my knowledge and skills in Data Science

Areas of interest

Data Mining

Data mining, to my understanding, combines data preparation, exploratory data analysis and modelling which pretty much sounds like data science. Thus, I am a fan! Whether wrangling data or implementing machine learning algorithms, I prefer R for all data mining purposes.

Data Visualization

The power that data visualization has in all domains coupled with its intricate, aesthetic beauty is what draws me to this field. As a true advocate of the Hadleyverse revolution, I love using ggplot2 for visualizations in R. Though, for interactivity purposes I am a huge Bostock fan and enjoy using D3.js

Applied Statistics

I am enthused by the realm of applied statistics having only taken a few upper-division statistics courses so far. With it's ubiquitous necessity in all sciences and the foundation it has laid for machine learning, I have a deep interest and respect for the power of statistics.
